3 unique things about chess

Strategic Depth and Complexity

Chess is known for its strategic depth, which comes from the possible positions and variations. The game's rules allow for a endless number of moves and strategies, leading to unique and unpredictable games.
Unique Piece Movements

Each piece has a way of moving, adding to the strategic possibilities of the game. The queen can move any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally, while the knight moves in an "L" shape, jumping over other pieces.
Special Moves

Chess uses special moves like castling (moving king and rook across), pawn promotion (turning a pawn to a another piece, not a king). These moves add further strategic layers and opportunities for good and complex plays.

How to begin a chess game

To begin a chess game, those who play white goes first and they can move their pieces. Pawns moves 2 spaces forward from the starting square then can only move one square. Rooks move up, down, left, right in an unlimited distance. The same goes for the bishops but only diagonally. The queen and knight was covered at the top in the 3 unique things section.
What is en passant in chess

The move en passant is like a passing move where when a player moves 2 spaces forward from their starting square and they move it next to one of your pawns you can take by moving that pawn behind the other one which is called en passant. It is a very rare move that is not played that often but only when there is a opportunity to play it but it is very optional to play that move.

Best Openings to play and learn

The best openings to play or learn when playing chess for white would be the "Scotch game", mainly to provide white with a quick attack plus the center and the "Queen's Gambit", this is mainly for being solid at the start to lead into other positions in the game. For black we have only the "Caro-Kann Defence" which is mostly for secure pawn structures and a counterplay.
